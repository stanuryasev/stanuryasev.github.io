CASE STUDY SUMMARYn Utility functions are quite popular in various financial applications. This case study compares portfolio optimization problems with Exponential, Logarithmic, and Linear-Quadratic utility functions. The rate of return dataset for a portfolio is provided for benchmarking purposes by EpiRisk Research company via Drs. Roger Wets and Michael Tian. The EpiRisk Research relies on letting the manager of a fixed-income portfolio solve a sequence of so-called tacking (optimization) models, described below, to shape the returns' distribution. The shape of the distribution is adjusted by selecting the coefficients of the appraisal (~ utility) function.
